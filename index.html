About Me

I am an Electronics and Communication Engineering student with a strong interest in building intelligent, real-world engineering systems. My work lies at the intersection of embedded hardware, wireless communication, VLSI design, artificial intelligence, and cloud technologies, where I focus on transforming theoretical concepts into practical solutions. I have hands-on experience in embedded systems development, including microcontroller programming, sensor interfacing, and hardware-software integration. Alongside this, I have a solid foundation in communication engineering, covering RF fundamentals, antennas, wireless channel modeling, and networking concepts, supported by extensive MATLAB-based simulations.

My academic and project experience also extends to VLSI design, where I work with CMOS digital circuits, flip-flops, latches, and radiation-tolerant architectures using industry-standard tools such as Cadence Virtuoso. To enhance system intelligence, I actively explore AI and machine learning techniques for decision-making, signal analysis, and automation, especially in applications involving embedded and communication systems. I also possess foundational knowledge of cloud computing and web technologies, enabling me to deploy, visualize, and manage data-driven systems effectively.

Overall, I aim to grow as a system-level engineer who can design hardware, understand signals, build reliable circuits, and integrate intelligence and connectivity to create impactful, scalable solutions.
